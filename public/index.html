No. 1 — clock + esp8266 · HN

Converting a $3.88 analog clock from Walmart into a ESP8266-based Wi-Fi clock

From the linkThe project transforms a simple $3.88 analog quartz clock into a Wi-Fi-enabled timepiece using an ESP8266 module. By employing an Arduino sketch, the device connects to a Network Time Protocol server, ensuring the clock reliably displays the accurate local time. To maintain precision, the ESP8266 updates time every 15 minutes and adjusts for daylight savings changes. This conversion offers a practical and low-cost approach to enhancing a traditional clock with modern technology, effectively merging functionality and simplicity.

From the commentsThe discussion surrounding the Wi-Fi clock project reveals a mix of curiosity and skepticism among participants. Some commenters question the project's ability to handle Daylight Saving Time adjustments effectively, while others express enthusiasm for the unique technologies involved, such as the SRAM with EEPROM backup chip. There is interest in similar projects, like converting different clock types, indicating a consensus that IoT enhancements to conventional clocks are appealing. However, some suggest alternatives, like NFC syncing, as potentially simpler solutions, showcasing a divide between those who advocate for Wi-Fi connectivity and those who see merit in lower-tech options.

No. 2 — backdoors + ivanti · HN

Sleeper Shells: Attackers Are Planting Dormant Backdoors in Ivanti EPMM

From the linkIn early February 2026, a new wave of exploitation targeting Ivanti Endpoint Manager Mobile (EPMM) emerged, characterized by a strategic shift in tactics. Unlike conventional methods that involve immediate exploitation, attackers uploaded a dormant in-memory Java class loader to a less common webshell path, /mifs/403.jsp. This approach allowed them to quietly establish persistent access without executing commands, merely confirming the payload's presence. This technique indicates a preliminary foothold, likely intended for later exploitation or resale, highlighting the evolving nature of threat strategies against major institutions.

From the commentsThe Hacker News comments reflect deep skepticism regarding Ivanti's security practices, with many viewing the company as negligent. There is a consensus that all Ivanti products pose significant threats, with strong criticism aimed at its failure to provide adequate explanations for recent exploits. Some commenters express amusement and concern over the vulnerabilities of enterprise management tools being turned into attack vectors, highlighting a broader fear of increased exploitation driven by advancements in AI and global tensions. The overall sentiment leans towards urgency for improved security measures and skepticism about the industry's reliance on compliance over true security.

No. 3 — sky + color · HN

Why Is the Sky Blue?

From the linkThe color of the sky is determined by the wavelengths of photons reaching our eyes, with light interacting in complex ways. While one might assume the sky could appear any color, the scattering of sunlight plays a pivotal role in its blue appearance. The process involves both pure frequencies of light and the mixture of multiple frequencies, which our brains interpret as a single color. Thus, the sky’s hue is a combination of physical properties and perceptual interpretation, highlighting the intricate relationship between light and our visual perception.

From the commentsThe feedback on the Hacker News thread demonstrates both consensus and disagreement surrounding the phenomenon of why the sky is blue. Many comments highlight the importance of structural colors in nature, such as blue butterflies, drawing parallels to Rayleigh scattering that explains the blueness of the sky. However, there is debate regarding specifics, particularly concerning why the sky doesn't appear green during sunsets, sparking curiosity about the underlying physics involved. Additionally, users shared resources, including lectures and comics, suggesting a shared interest in deepening understanding while acknowledging varying interpretations of the scientific concepts involved.

No. 4 — programming + c · HN

Thoughts on Generating C

From the linkThe author reflects on the nuances of generating C, noting that while it may be less hazardous than writing C directly, certain practices can enhance the process. Drawing from experience in compilers, the author emphasizes the benefits of using inline functions over preprocessor macros for more reliable data handling, which reduces performance penalties. This insight, gained over time, underlines the importance of structured approaches to avoid pitfalls commonly associated with C programming. The piece conveys an experienced perspective, offering practical strategies for effective code generation.

From the commentsIn the Hacker News discussion about generating C code, several commenters expressed a consensus regarding the complexities of using C as a backend, particularly around garbage collection and debugging. Many highlighted the challenges with reliably implementing features like certain attributes across major compilers and the difficulties in managing accurate stack-tracing for garbage collection, suggesting potential solutions such as using shadow stacks. While some respondents shared personal experiences with similar compiler projects and workarounds to improve debugging, there was noticeable disagreement on the best approaches and whether a strict subset of C or a simpler language should be targeted. Overall, the comments reflect a mix of enthusiasm for the potential of generating C alongside practical concerns and technical challenges.

No. 5 — uefi + javascript · HN

UEFI Bindings for JavaScript

From the linkThe Promethee project introduces UEFI bindings for JavaScript, functioning as a proof of concept that demonstrates how JavaScript can interact with UEFI services. It allows for the execution of boot scripts loaded directly from a UEFI FAT volume, with the primary entry point being a file named script.js. Built with support from tools like Duktape and Node.js, this project streamlines the process of creating bootloaders using JavaScript, minimizing dependencies by providing only essential libc stubs. This innovative approach offers a glimpse into utilizing familiar web technologies in the realm of system firmware.

From the commentsThe comments on the UEFI Bindings for JavaScript exhibit a mix of enthusiasm and skepticism regarding the project’s approach. Many commenters appreciate the idea of exposing raw EFI protocols directly to JavaScript, highlighting the potential for rapid prototyping without obscuring hardware access. However, there are underlying tensions between those who believe JavaScript should remain confined to traditional environments and those advocating for its broader application, including filesystem access and operating system development. While some view the effort as innovative and potentially impactful, others express caution, suggesting it may be more of a novelty than a practical solution for production environments.

No. 6 — algorithm + visibility · HN

Show HN: Algorithmically Finding the Longest Line of Sight on Earth

From the linkA custom-developed algorithm, CacheTVS, has been used to identify the longest line of sight on Earth, which spans 530 kilometers from the Hindu Kush to Pik Dankova. The analysis reveals that the longest lines of sight often converge around peaks and ridges. In addition to the primary finding, a curated selection of notable lines of sight was compiled, showcasing significant views from diverse global regions. Among these, the second longest measures 504 kilometers, connecting Antioquia to Pico Crostobal in Colombia. This exploration provides a unique perspective on Earth's geological vistas.

From the commentsThe Hacker News discussion around the project on finding the longest line of sight on Earth reflects a mix of enthusiasm and skepticism among commenters. Several participants expressed admiration for the project's ingenuity, suggesting enhancements like 3D visualizations to better convey the data. However, there were notable disagreements regarding the accuracy of the reported distances and the impact of atmospheric conditions on the findings. Some commenters raised concerns about the reliability of specific data points, questioning the static nature of the "world record" given the influence of weather. Overall, while the project's novelty was appreciated, technical discrepancies and visualization suggestions were prominent topics of contention.
